Why It Matters

The Hidden Cost of an Unserviced Oil Boiler

Most oil heating problems do not arrive with a bang. They build quietly — soot in the heat exchanger, a tired pump, a corroded tank — until a cold morning turns into a no-heat emergency, a failed sale or a denied insurance claim.

Carbon Monoxide Risk

A poorly burning boiler can spill carbon monoxide back into your home. An OFTEC service measures combustion CO and CO2 directly and confirms the flue is sealed and drawing properly.

Voided Manufacturer Warranty

Grant, Worcester Bosch, Firebird, Warmflow and Trianco all require an annual service by an OFTEC registered engineer. Skip a year and the manufacturer can refuse to honour the warranty on a £4k+ boiler.

Insurance Claims Refused

Home insurers increasingly ask whether the oil installation has been serviced annually and whether the tank meets BS 5410. A negative answer can be enough to reduce or refuse a claim after a leak or fire.

Higher Fuel Bills

An unserviced burner runs out of tune. A 5% drop in efficiency on a 1,000-litre annual usage is roughly £70 wasted at current kerosene prices — far more than the cost of a service.

Landlord Compliance Gap

Landlords letting properties with oil heating must keep evidence of an annual safety check on the appliance and tank. A landlord oil safety record protects you in a deposit dispute and at insurance renewal.

Sale Delayed by the Tank

Surveyors flag old single-skin tanks, undersized bunds and short fire-separation distances. Catching this before the buyer's surveyor does keeps the sale on track and the price intact.

An annual service costs less than one no-heat callout in January.

Self-Check

Signs Your Oil Boiler or Tank Needs Attention

Not sure if you are due a service? If any of the following apply, book an OFTEC inspection.

  • !
    It has been over 12 months since the last service

    Manufacturer warranties and most home insurance policies expect annual servicing.

  • !
    The boiler is sooting up, smoking or smelling of oil

    Yellow flame, soot at the flue terminal or an oil smell near the boiler all point to bad combustion.

  • !
    The boiler keeps locking out

    Frequent lockouts usually mean a clogged nozzle, a tired pump or an air ingress on the supply line.

  • !
    You are buying or selling a property with oil heating

    A pre-purchase oil heating inspection avoids surprises at survey and gives you a paper trail at exchange.

  • !
    The oil tank is single-skin, more than 10 years old, or shows staining

    BS 5410 requires bunding for tanks above certain capacities and within 10m of a building. Aged tanks split without warning.

  • !
    You own an AGA, Rayburn or Stanley range

    Range cookers run almost continuously and need annual deep-cleaning of the burner, flueways and heat exchanger.
